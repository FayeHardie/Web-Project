Different species of sharks

Blue Shark Prionace glauca

The blue shark is an open-ocean shark (pelagic) species that visits the UK seas in the summer. They are active predators and feed mainly on small fish and squid, although they are also known to eat sea birds and other small sharks. They have been recorded reaching depths of up to 350m to feed. The largest blue shark caught measured to be over 9ft (2.74m).

Greenland Shark Somniosus microcephalus

The greenland shark is a member of the sleeper shark family. The species is primarily found in cold waters such as the Arctic Ocean and the North Atlantic, their range also extends to the North Sea. The species can reach a length of 23ft (7m) when fully grown but most are between 6.5ft and 13ft. The life span of this creature can be as long as 500 years.

Horn Shark Heterodontus francisci

Horn sharks are not the most graceful of swimmers and tend to stick to the sea floor, they have been seen using their pectoral fins to crawl along rocks. They can be found in kelp forests and their diet consists of crustaceans, sea urchins and small fishes. Females lay spiral egg cases as seen in the image.

Megamouth Shark Megachasma pelagios

The megamouth shark is a filter feeder, it is the smallest species of filter feeders. They live near the surface to as deep as 15,000 feet (4600m). Although it is one of the largest sharks in the world, this species was only discovered in 1976. The first known megamouth shark was accidentally captured when it became entangled in a sea anchor.

Tope Shark Galeorhinus galeus

The tope shark has other known names such as: School Shark, Oil Shark, Penny's Dog and Miller's Dog. They can reach up to 6 feet long their diet consists of smaller fish, squid and crustaceans. They are found in British waters but are more common in western coasts of the British Isles.
