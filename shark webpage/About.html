What is a shark?


Sharks belong to the cartilaginous fish group. This means fish that have a simple internal skeleton formed by cartilage.


Body structure of a shark.


The basic body structure of a shark has remained the same for millions of years.



  • Caudal fin
    they are thought to provide stability whilst swimming.
  • Dorsal fins
    in the majority of sharks, the first is larger than the second (if there is more than one).
  • Pelvic fins
    in males these have claspers (claspers - male reproductive anatomical structure) with them.
  • Pectoral fins
    they produce lift and help the sharks control swimming.
  • Gill slits
    majority of sharks have 5 however a few species have 6 or 7!
  • Spiracles
    they are found behind the eye in most sharks, largely found in bottom-dwelling sharks. Spiracles are used to take in water and ventilate the gills even while the shark could be feeding or resting.
  • Eyes
    most species have moveable eyelids, others have protective eyelids called 'nictitating' lower eyelids.
  • Ampullae of Lorenzini
    sensitive electrical field detectors, they are tiny black spots, and are extremely important for foraging and navigation.
